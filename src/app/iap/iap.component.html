Indigo Art Project
Let the art do the talking
The Indigo Art Project is an initiative by Mood Indigo, IIT Bombay, with a motive to provide a platform for you to take your creativity to another dimension and to give voice to your ideas. This is an opportunity for you to present your art to the world and work along artists from around the world. Through this project we’ll together make MI more exciting, interactive and appealing.
What will you do?
What will you do?
You will create beautiful art pieces and get an opportunity to present your art in Mood Indigo’s own Art Gallery. You will also get to work in the ideation and fabrication of the ambience elements for the fest, and further in the execution of the ambience plan for the fest with experienced artists. There is a range of ambience elements that you could work on such as On-ground installations, Interactive art, Aerial ambience, Stage design and a lot more
What will you get?
What will you get?

You’ll get to attend workshops from various artists covering a range of art styles including digital arts, visual arts, fine arts and many more. For completing the project, you will get certificates signed by Dean, IIT Bombay.

Your work will be an excellent addition to your portfolio and will be encountered by all the people joining us at the festival.You will also learn skills like Teamwork, Networking, Negotiation and a lot more.

Additionally, you will get VIP Concert passes, free accommodation during the IAP and many more exciting rewards.

I’m Arnav Agarwal, a UX Design student, I belong to a creative field and my job is to create meaningful experiences for the users in order to solve their problems. Most of the times it so happens that you do not get to explore the artist inside you as there’s an end user. Indigo Art Project was a place where I got to explore a lot more than just creating wonderful interactions and experiences for the Mood Indigo crowd, here I got to work for creating various interaction zones and ambiences and got a chance to think creatively about how to keep the audiences engaged without facing much limitations. For an example, we were to print the registration desk cover and there were some random hand drawn shapes combined as an artwork and what I did was, I tried seeing the visual design in terms of user experience and found that since people wait in a queue for getting themselves registered, I replaced some of the shapes with the MI logo and then the user’s task was to find the number of logo present in the entire art board, it helped in keeping the users engaged as well as solved the purpose without extra cost. I truly had an amazing experience while working for the Indigo Art Project. As I always say, Mood Indigo vibes are magical.

Arnav,

MIT Institute of Design,

Pune
